      1. One of the three famous ancient Tamil dynasties (other two being Chola and Chera), the Chola dynasty ruled several parts of the Southern India until the 15th century CE. Their main capital was thanjai, from where they ruled their country chola Nadu. The Chola kingdom grew under him into an extensive and well-knit empire, efficiently organized and administered and possessing a powerful standing army and navy. Rajaraja began his conquests by attacking the confederation between the rulers of the Pandya and Kerala kingdoms and of Ceylon. Polonnaruva became the capital of Chola province in North Ceylon after the defeat of Mahinda V, the Ceylonese king.
